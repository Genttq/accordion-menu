Frequently Asked Questions

The Moon is sometimes visible during the day because of its orbit around the Earth, which positions it above the horizon during daylight hours. It reflects sunlight, making it bright enough to see even against the blue sky. The visibility depends on the lunar phase, particularly during the first and last quarters. The Moon's brightness contrasts with the daytime sky, allowing it to be seen.

The sky appears blue due to a phenomenon known as Rayleigh scattering. This occurs because molecules and small particles in the Earth's atmosphere scatter sunlight in all directions.

The question of whether we will ever discover aliens is both intriguing and complex, involving numerous scientific, technological, and philosophical considerations. While it's impossible to predict the future with certainty, several factors influence the likelihood of discovering extraterrestrial life.

The Earth weighs approximately 5,970,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 kilograms. This immense mass is determined through gravitational calculations, observations of orbital mechanics, and experiments like the Cavendish experiment. The Earth's mass is essential for understanding gravitational forces and interactions with other celestial bodies. Its weight reflects the planet's size and density.

Airplanes stay up due to the balance of four main forces. Lift, created by the wing shape, counteracts weight. Thrust from the engines propels the airplane forward, enabling lift. Drag opposes this motion, and control surfaces help pilots manage direction and stability. By balancing lift, weight, thrust, and drag, airplanes achieve and maintain flight.
